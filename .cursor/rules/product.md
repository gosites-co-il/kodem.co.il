# Kodem Product Definition

Version: 1.0

---

# Mission

Kodem helps business owners understand their business, make better decisions, and continuously improve using AI.

The platform learns the business over time, builds a Business Knowledge Model (BKM), and transforms raw information into actionable insights and recommendations.

---

# Vision

Every business should have an AI Business Partner.

Not a chatbot.

Not a reporting tool.

Not another CRM.

An intelligent system that understands the business and proactively helps it grow.

---

# Product Philosophy

Kodem is not feature-driven.

Kodem is knowledge-driven.

Every action performed inside the platform should increase the platform's understanding of the business.

Knowledge compounds over time.

The longer a business uses Kodem, the smarter the platform becomes.

---

# Core Principles

## Business First

Everything revolves around the business.

Not around users.

Not around modules.

Not around databases.

The Workspace represents the business.

---

## Business Knowledge is the Core Asset

Business Knowledge is more valuable than raw data.

Raw data is temporary.

Knowledge becomes part of the business memory.

Every module contributes to Business Knowledge.

Every engine consumes and enriches Business Knowledge.

---

## AI Assists, It Does Not Decide

AI generates observations, insights and recommendations.

Business rules always remain deterministic.

The user always has the final decision.

---

## Event Driven

Everything meaningful generates an Event.

Events become the timeline of the business.

Events feed the Engines.

Events improve the Business Knowledge Model.

---

## Progressive Intelligence

The first day Kodem knows very little.

Every interaction improves the Business Knowledge Model.

Every connected integration improves understanding.

Every recommendation improves future recommendations.

---

## Self-Service First

The product is PLG.

Users should discover value with minimal setup.

The onboarding experience should generate the first Business Knowledge automatically.

---

## Modular Platform

Capabilities are delivered through Modules.

Core modules are always available.

Premium modules extend the platform.

Modules never own the business.

They provide capabilities to the Workspace.

---

# Product Structure

Kodem consists of four layers.

Platform

↓

Workspace

↓

Business Knowledge

↓

Modules

The user interacts with modules.

Modules interact with Business Knowledge.

Business Knowledge is maintained by Engines.

---

# Platform

The Platform provides shared capabilities.

Examples:

- Authentication
- Subscription Management
- AI Gateway
- Module Catalog
- Integration Catalog
- Feature Flags
- Logging
- Monitoring
- Usage Tracking

---

# Workspace

A Workspace represents a business.

Everything inside Kodem belongs to a Workspace.

A Workspace owns:

- Business Profile
- Members
- Permissions
- Business Knowledge
- Events
- Assets
- Module Configuration
- AI Configuration

---

# Modules

Modules provide business capabilities.

Examples:

Core Modules

- CRM
- Digital Card

Premium Modules

- Marketing
- Campaign Management
- Insights
- Automation
- Booking

Modules never become the source of truth.

Business Knowledge is always the source of truth.

---

# Business Knowledge Model (BKM)

The Business Knowledge Model represents Kodem's understanding of a business.

It evolves continuously.

It stores:

- Facts
- Relationships
- Goals
- Audience
- Products
- Services
- Observations
- Patterns
- Hypotheses
- Insights
- Recommendations

The BKM is the intelligence layer of the platform.

---

# Engines

Engines continuously improve the Business Knowledge Model.

Current Engines:

- Discovery Engine
- Learning Engine
- Insight Engine
- Recommendation Engine
- Rule Engine

Future engines can be added without changing the platform architecture.

---

# Success Metric

The success of Kodem is NOT measured by:

- Number of features
- Number of reports
- Number of dashboards

The success of Kodem is measured by:

- Better business understanding
- Better business decisions
- Better business outcomes

---

# Golden Rule

Whenever implementing a new feature ask:

"Does this improve the platform's understanding of the business?"

If the answer is no, reconsider the implementation.
