# Kodem Glossary

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Draft

---

# Purpose

This glossary defines the official language used across the Kodem platform.

Every document, API, UI, database model, AI prompt and source code must use the terminology defined here.

Each business concept should have one canonical name.

---

# Core Domain

## Workspace

The highest-level entity in the platform.

A Workspace represents a single customer account and serves as the container for all business data.

Each Workspace has its own:

* Users
* Business Profile
* Modules
* Integrations
* Data
* Settings
* Subscription

Example:

```
acme.kodem.app
```

---

## Workspace Owner

The user who created the Workspace.

The Workspace Owner has full administrative access, including billing, subscription management and user management.

---

## Member

A user who belongs to a Workspace.

Members receive permissions through one or more Roles.

---

## Role

A predefined collection of permissions assigned to Members.

Examples:

* Owner
* Manager
* Employee

---

## Permission

A specific capability granted to a Role.

Examples:

* Create Lead
* Edit Customer
* Manage Users
* Publish Digital Card

---

# Business

## Business Profile

The structured representation of a business.

The Business Profile describes how the business operates and serves as the primary business context within the platform.

Typical information includes:

* Business details
* Industry
* Services
* Sales process
* Communication preferences
* Business hours
* Service areas
* Brand information
* Website
* Social channels

The Business Profile evolves throughout the lifetime of the Workspace.

---

## Business Assessment

The onboarding experience responsible for creating the initial Business Profile.

The assessment combines information gathered from:

* Website analysis
* User input
* Dynamic questions
* Connected systems

Its purpose is to establish the first understanding of the business.

---

# Customer Management

## Lead

A person or organization that has expressed interest in the business.

Typical Lead sources include:

* Website
* Digital Card
* Landing Page
* QR Code
* Google
* Meta
* Referral
* Manual entry
* API

A Lead may later become a Customer.

---

## Contact

A person known by the business.

A Contact may exist without becoming a Customer.

---

## Customer

A Contact who has completed at least one successful business transaction.

---

## Company

A business entity associated with one or more Leads, Contacts or Customers.

---

# Work Management

## Task

A unit of work assigned to one or more Members.

Tasks may be created manually or automatically.

---

## Activity

A meaningful interaction related to a business object.

Examples include:

* Phone call
* Email
* WhatsApp conversation
* Meeting
* Note

Activities represent user interactions.

---

# Platform

## Module

A major functional area of the platform.

Examples:

* CRM
* Digital Card
* Automation
* Marketing
* Analytics
* AI Assistant

Modules may be included in a Subscription or offered as optional add-ons.

A Module contains one or more Features.

---

## Feature

A specific capability provided by a Module.

Examples:

CRM Module:

* Lead Management
* Pipeline
* Tasks

Marketing Module:

* Campaign Dashboard
* UTM Builder
* ROI Analysis

---

## Subscription

The commercial plan assigned to a Workspace.

A Subscription determines:

* Available Modules
* Available Features
* Usage limits
* User limits
* AI quotas
* Storage limits

---

## Connector

A reusable integration with an external platform.

Examples:

* Google
* Meta
* WhatsApp
* Gmail
* Microsoft 365
* Stripe

---

## Integration

A configured Connector attached to a specific Workspace.

The Integration contains the Workspace-specific configuration required to communicate with an external platform.

---

## Event

A recorded business or system occurrence.

Examples:

* Lead Created
* Lead Updated
* Task Completed
* Meeting Scheduled
* Form Submitted
* Integration Connected

Events provide historical context for the platform.

---

# Intelligence

## Observation

A fact identified by the platform.

Observations may contribute to future Insights.

Example:

> Average response time increased by 18%.

---

## Hypothesis

A temporary assumption generated by the platform.

A Hypothesis represents something that appears to be true but still requires validation.

Example:

> Customers arriving from Google convert better than customers arriving from Facebook.

---

## Confidence

A numerical representation of certainty associated with an Observation, Hypothesis or Insight.

Confidence may change as additional information becomes available.

---

## Insight

A meaningful conclusion generated from business information.

Insights help explain what is happening in the business.

Insights may combine:

* Business Profile
* Events
* Historical information
* Connected systems
* AI analysis
* Rule evaluation

---

## Recommendation

A suggested action generated by the platform.

Recommendations help users decide what to do next.

A Recommendation may originate from:

* An Insight
* AI analysis
* Business rules

---

## Business Knowledge

The accumulated understanding of a Workspace.

Business Knowledge consists of:

* Business Profile
* Observations
* Hypotheses
* Insights
* Historical patterns
* Learned preferences

Business Knowledge continuously evolves throughout the lifetime of the Workspace.

---

# Automation

## Trigger

An occurrence capable of starting an automation.

---

## Workflow

A sequence of automated Actions executed after a Trigger.

---

## Action

A single executable step performed by a Workflow.

Examples:

* Create Task
* Send Email
* Send WhatsApp
* Update Status
* Call Webhook

---

## Rule

A business condition evaluated by the platform.

Rules are used to generate automations, validations and insights.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

## AI Observation

Knowledge inferred by AI from available business information.

AI Observations contribute to Business Knowledge.

---

## Learning

The continuous process through which the platform improves its understanding of a business over time.

Learning is based on user activity, connected systems and historical business information.

---

# Digital Presence

## Digital Card

The public-facing digital identity of a business.

A Digital Card may function as:

* Digital business card
* Mini website
* Landing page
* Lead generation page

Digital Cards belong to a Workspace and may generate Leads and business activity.

---

## Landing Page

A public page designed to capture Leads for a specific campaign, service or purpose.

Landing Pages may exist independently of the Digital Card.

---

# Analytics

## Dashboard

The primary workspace screen presenting the current state of the business.

The Dashboard combines business information, insights, recommendations and operational metrics.

---

## Metric

A measurable business value.

Examples:

* New Leads
* Close Rate
* Response Time
* Revenue
* Conversion Rate

---

## KPI

A Key Performance Indicator used to evaluate business performance against predefined objectives.
